Frequently Asked Questions about Company Registration in Zimbabwe

You can register various company types in Zimbabwe, including:
Private Limited Company (PLC): The most common type, offering limited liability to its shareholders.
Private Business Corporation (PBC): A simpler, less formal structure often preferred by small businesses and sole traders due to reduced compliance requirements.
Public Limited Company (Pty) Ltd: Suitable for larger businesses that intend to raise capital from the public.
Close Corporation (CC): (Though less common now, still exists) A simpler form for small businesses, typically with fewer members.
Non-Profit Organisation/Trust/Private Voluntary Organisation (PVO): For charitable, educational, or other non-profit purposes.

Yes it is a mandatory requirement to have a physical registered address in Zimbabwe for your company. This address is where all official communication and legal documents will be sent. If you don't have one, some company registration service providers offer a virtual office or registered address service.

Registering with the Zimbabwe Revenue Authority (ZIMRA): for a Business Partner Number (BPN), Income Tax, VAT (if applicable), PAYE (if you employ staff).
NSSA Registration: Registering with the National Social Security Authority.
Annual Returns: Filing annual returns with the Companies Office.
Maintenance of Records: Keeping proper accounting records, minutes of meetings, and registers.
Audits: Depending on the company type and turnover, annual audits may be required.

Yes foreign nationals can register companies in Zimbabwe. The requirements are largely similar to those for local residents, but foreign directors/shareholders will need to provide certified copies of their passports and proof of foreign address. There might also be specific regulations regarding foreign investment, which a consultant can advise on.
